The Origin of Chess: A Journey Through Time

Chess, known as "the game of kings," has a rich history dating back thousands of years. Although its exact origin is still debated, it is believed that chess originated in India around the 6th century with the game called "chaturanga," which was played on an 8x8 board and reflected the different branches of the military: infantry, cavalry, elephants, and chariots.

Chess in the Islamic World and Europe

After its spread to the Arab world, chess was introduced to Europe in the Middle Ages, where it evolved into the game we know today. During the 15th and 16th centuries, the rules of chess were standardized, and the first international competitions began to be held.

Great Champions and Key Moments

From the legendary figure of Bobby Fischer to the modern era with Magnus Carlsen, chess has produced champions who have left an indelible mark on the history of the game. Each tournament, each match, and each play has marked an important step in the evolution of this strategic game.

Cronology

Year / Period Event
6th Century Chess originates in India under the name *chaturanga*.
7th–8th Century Chess spreads to the Islamic world and then to Europe.
15th Century Modern chess begins to take shape in Europe; the queen and bishop gain enhanced mobility.
1851 The first international chess tournament is held in London.
1886 Wilhelm Steinitz becomes the first official World Chess Champion.
1924 The International Chess Federation (FIDE) is founded.
1972 Bobby Fischer defeats Boris Spassky to become World Champion, a historic Cold War moment.
1997 IBM's Deep Blue defeats reigning world champion Garry Kasparov.
2023 Ding Liren becomes World Chess Champion after defeating Ian Nepomniachtchi.
